Brand Analysis: Visual Merchandising — December 2025

What makes a good brand? How do companies form a brand identity? How does brand identity affect customer retention and cult-like loyalty? This research assignment examines Le Majordome NYC — a Swiss luxury shoemaker founded in 2012 — as a case study in intentional small-company design, value-based pricing, and community-driven marketing.

About the Project

Le Majordome is a Swiss luxury shoemaker whose name translates to "the butlers" in French. With only two full-time employees and a single partner factory in Almansa, Spain, the brand demonstrates that intentional small-scale design can create genuine luxury. Ready-to-wear models range from $460–$750, with fully bespoke options available. Rather than competing on price, the brand competes on a fully personalized experience: custom fit, handcrafted construction, and one-on-one expert guidance. Their marketing strategy is hyperlocal and relationship-driven — guerrilla outreach to neighboring Madison Avenue retailers, exclusive community events, and earned media coverage in Bloomberg Businessweek and Highsnobiety. The Bloomberg feature alone drove a measurable spike in sales, revealing their core customer as the Midtown office worker.
